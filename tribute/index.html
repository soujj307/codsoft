Mr. Ratan Tata

The man who changed lifes of several indians

Time line of Mr. Ratan Tata's life:

  • 1937 - Born in Mumbai, India
  • 1955 - Graduated from Riverdale Country School in New York City.
  • 1959 - He then received a degree in Architecture from Cornell University.
  • 1971 - He gained experience in a number of Tata Group businesses and was named director in charge
  • 1991 - He became chairman of Tata Industries
  • 2000 - Acquired London-based Tetley Tea for $431.3 million.
  • 2004 - Purchased the truck-manufacturing operations of South Korea’s Daewoo Motors for $102 million.
  • 2007 - Tata Steel completed the biggest corporate takeover by an Indian company when it acquired the giant Anglo-Dutch steel manufacturer Corus Group for $11.3 billion.
  • 2008 - Tata oversaw Tata Motors’ purchase of the elite British car brands Jaguar and Land Rover from the Ford Motor Company. The $2.3 billion deal marked the largest-ever acquisition by an Indian automotive firm. & received Padma Vibhushan
  • 2009 - Company launched the Tata nano, a tiny pod-shaped vehicle with a starting price of 1 lakh INR[$2,000]. The highly touted “People’s Car” could seat up to five adults and, in Tata’s words, would provide a “safe, affordable, all-weather form of transport” to millions of middle- and lower-income consumers both in India and abroad.
  • 2012 - Tata retired as chairman of the Tata Group.
  • 2016 - He briefly served as interim chairman following the ouster of his successor, Cyrus Mistry.
  • 2017 - Tata returned to retirement when Natarajan Chandrasekaran was appointed chairman of the Tata Group.
